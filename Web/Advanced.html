How to do a Jelly Lay-up?

Step 1: Approach the Basket Start by driving towards the hoop at a good speed, just like you would for a normal lay-up. Keep control of the ball and your balance.

Step 2: Take Off Jump off one foot (usually the opposite foot of your shooting hand). For example, if you’re shooting with your right hand, jump off your left foot.

Step 3: Twist Your Wrist Here’s where the “jelly” comes in: instead of a normal lay-up where you push the ball softly off the backboard, you add extra wrist movement. Roll your wrist so the ball spins and “floats” as it heads toward the rim.

Step 4: Add Style The jelly is all about flair. You can finish with your shooting hand underneath the ball, then flick and twist it for a smooth spin. Some players even add a little hang time by switching hands mid-air.

Step 5: Finish Guide the ball into the basket with a soft touch. The key is the spin—it makes the shot look stylish while still being effective.

Drills

Drill 1: Regular Lay-Up Warm-Up Do 10 normal lay-ups with each hand. Focus on balance, footwork (jumping off the opposite foot), and finishing softly. 👉 Purpose: Build a strong foundation before adding flair.

Drill 2: Wrist Roll Practice Stand close to the hoop. Toss the ball softly against the backboard using only your wrist flick (no jump). Do 20 reps with each hand. 👉 Purpose: Train the “jelly” spin and touch.

Drill 3: Slow Jelly Lay-Up Drive to the hoop at half-speed. Jump and add the wrist roll before releasing the ball. Do 10 reps per hand. 👉 Purpose: Learn the motion under control.

Drill 4: Full-Speed Jelly Do a normal drive at game speed. Jump, twist your wrist, and finish with the jelly spin. Alternate hands (10 reps each side). 👉 Purpose: Add speed and real-game feel.

Drill 5: Freestyle & Creativity Try different angles (baseline, middle, reverse). Experiment with switching hands mid-air or adding extra spin. Do at least 15 creative attempts. 👉 Purpose: Build confidence and style.

⚡ Training Tip: Focus on soft touch + high arc. Don’t worry if you miss at first—the jelly is about both flair and control.
